2  Overview of Practices

2.1 Table of “Goodness”

We create a table of practice information

  • occurrence Percent of students reporting the practice occurred
  • impact_rating Mean rating (1-5) of how impactful the practice was IF they said practice occurred
  • high_impact Percent of students who put the practice was “Extremely Important” (5 of 5)
  • frequency_more_than_1_per_month Percent of the practice being frequent (“About 2-3 times per month” or “At least 1 time per week”)
  • occurrence_this_week Percent of students reporting it had occurred in the past week.
  • occurrence_inst_report Percent of instructors reporting “Yes”
  • occurence_inst_NO Percent of students reporting (if instructor “No”) -
  • occurence_inst_YES Percent of students reporting (if instructor “Yes”) -
  • occurence_inst_signal Difference in student reporting when instructor says YES or NO
  • ICC Intraclass Correlation Coefficient
  • Consistency
  • F-value

2.3 Ranking items

We also sort based on what makes a “good” item. I used the following criteria as a first pass: - students find impactful Do students find the practice impactful? (<25th %ile = 0 point; 25th-75th %ile = 1 points, 75-90%ile = 2 points; >90%ile = 3 points) - Is it significantly more important to marginalized students? (No = 0 points; Yes = 2 points) - is there consistency of student-reports within courses? (<25th %ile = 0 point; 25th-75th %ile = 1 points, >75%ile = 2 points) - is there a signal across courses? (non-significant F-value = 0 point; <75%ile sig F-value = 1 point, >75%ile = 2 points) - does it predict student experiences? - students want instructors to prioritize (NEED TO ADD IN IMPORTANCE) - signal by instructor Are there positive differences in student reports when instructors do or do not report a practice? (<= 0 difference = -1 points; 0-.10 = 0 points; .10-.20 = 1 point; .20-.30 = 2 points; .30-.40 = 4 points; etc.) - In what percent of courses is there significant change?* p<.05 and shift of > 10% in student responses. ( Determine criteria)
